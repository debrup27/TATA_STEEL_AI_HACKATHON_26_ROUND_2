## Part 5: Standards Documents and Guidance

Below are key standards relevant to steel rolling mills and asset management:

- **ISO 55000:2024 – Asset Management (Overview & Principles)** – ISO (International Organization for Standardization). Defines asset management concepts and benefits. [Purchase from ISO](https://www.iso.org/standard/83053.html). *Scope:* Vocabulary, principles of asset management.  
  *Guidance:* Summaries available from SMRP/PI (e.g. [SMRP ISO 5500x Overview](https://smrp.org/asset-management/)).  
- **ISO 55001:2020 – Asset Management (Management Systems)** – ISO. Specifies requirements for an asset management system. (Related: ISO 55002 guidance.) [Purchase](https://www.iso.org/standard/55088.html).  
- **ISO 14224:2016 – Reliability Data (Petrochemicals)** – ISO. Standard format for collecting reliability and maintenance (RM) data in oil, gas, chemical industries. [ISO Page](https://www.iso.org/standard/64076.html).  
- **ISO 17359:2018 – Condition Monitoring & Diagnostics** – ISO. Provides a framework for establishing condition-based monitoring (CBM) programs. (Focus on CBM planning and review.) [ISO Page](https://www.iso.org/standard/67097.html).  
- **ISO 13373 / ISO 20816 – Vibration Analysis** – ISO. Guidelines on vibration monitoring of machinery (ISO 13373) and vibration severity (ISO 20816). PDFs not free; summaries exist via AI/ASQ.  
- **IEC 61508 / IEC 61511 – Functional Safety** – IEC. Basic standards for safety instrumented systems (SIS) in machinery. (61508 is generic, 61511 for process industry).  
- **ISO 9001:2015 – Quality Management** – ISO. (General QMS standard; included for completeness.)  
- **OSHA 1910.119 – Process Safety Management (PSM)** – OSHA (USA). Covers safety management for hazardous processes (including high-temp furnaces, descalers). [OSHA Text](https://www.osha.gov/laws-regs/regulations/standardnumber/1910/1910.119).  
- **OSHA 1910.269 – Electric Power Generation/Distribution** – OSHA. Safety for power and electrical equipment (covers rolling mill electric systems).  
- **API 610 – Centrifugal Pumps** – API. Standard for centrifugal pump design (relevant for mill cooling systems).  
- **ASTM A123 / A153 – Coatings (Hot-dip galvanizing)** – ASTM. (For protecting steel surfaces, e.g. pickling line tanks.)  
- **ISO 12100 – Machine Safety (Risk Assessment)** – ISO. Guides hazard analysis and risk reduction in machine design.  

Each of these standards can be obtained from the official ISO/IEC/ASTM websites (purchase required). Free summaries are available on engineering forums or organizations’ sites (e.g. NIST, AIAG). For example, ISO 14224 has a free abstract onlineand outlines reliability data practice.

## Part 7: Generated SOP & Maintenance Documents

For completeness, we outline industry-standard procedure templates (to be inserted into your MD):

- **Startup SOP (Horizon Hot Mill Furnace):**  
  1. **Pre-Startup Checks:** Verify all safety interlocks and gauges are normal. Ensure furnaces water-cooled doors are closed, blowdown vents sealed, burners in purge mode.  
  2. **Utility Preparations:** Ramp up forced draft fans and burner blowers. Confirm fuel (gas or oil) supply valves are open. Check CO/O2 sensors are operational.  
  3. **Sequenced Lighting:** Open natural gas valves, ignite ignition burners to preheat; gradually introduce furnace combustion. Monitor flame scanners.  
  4. **Walk-Beam/Pusher Activation:** Once temperature reaches ~400°C, start slab transport (walking beams or pusher). Ensure slab positions by sensors before full heating.  
  5. **Ramp to Operating Temp:** Increase burners to full, stabilizing temperature at ~1100–1150°C. Balance fuel/air for uniform heat.  
  6. **Instrumentation Checks:** Verify temperature distribution, pressure, flow readings are in range. Log initial readings.  
  *(Citing sources: modern furnace manuals specify slow heating to avoid thermal shock. Typical procedure described in industry SOPs.)*

- **Shutdown SOP (Horizon Hot Mill Furnace):**  
  1. **Cease Charging:** Do not add new slabs. Allow all slabs to exit the furnace on the roller hearth.  
  2. **Fuel Ramp-Down:** Gradually reduce burner firing rate, lower exhaust gas temperature in stages (e.g. 50°C per minute) to avoid thermal stress.  
  3. **Maintain Fans & Purge:** Keep combustion air fans running to purge gases. Inject nitrogen to inert during cool-down if required.  
  4. **Complete Cool-Down:** When furnace cools below ~400°C, shut off burners entirely.  
  5. **Water Cooling:** Continue running cooling bed/cooling lines to bring slabs to safe handling temperature.  
  6. **Lockout:** Verify furnace is offline; follow lockout/tagout for furnace burner valves and electrical panels.  
  *(Based on standard furnace shutdown practice.)*

- **Emergency Procedure (General):**  
  1. **Alarm/Emergency Stop:** If any critical fault (e.g. flameout, overtemp, spill) occurs, hit local E-Stop or alarm.  
  2. **Isolation:** Activate safety interlocks (gas shutoff, hydraulic kill switches).  
  3. **Vent/Cool:** Open flue vents, ensure forced air fans purge the equipment.  
  4. **Evacuation:** If fire or explosion risk, evacuate area per plant emergency plan.  
  *(References: Typical emergency sections in process plant SOPs.)*

- **Daily Inspection Checklist (Example):**  
  - Check lubrication levels (gearboxes, bearing oil reservoirs).  
  - Inspect critical oil/air filters; clean or replace if dirty.  
  - Verify water cooling flows and valves.  
  - Walk gearbox/Vfd rooms; note unusual noise or vibration.  
  - Inspect electrical cabinets for overheating (via thermal camera).  
  *(These items align with SKF and API maintenance guides.)*

- **Weekly Maintenance Checklist (Example):**  
  - Perform vibration trend analysis on gearboxes/motors.  
  - Inspect roll mill alignment and tighten bolts.  
  - Check hydraulic cylinder rod seals for leakage, top up reservoir.  
  - Test spare safety/limit switches for functionality.  
  *(Derived from RCM best practices.)*

- **Monthly Maintenance:**  
  - Change lubricant in main mill drives and gearbox according to manufacturer spec.  
  - Check refractory integrity in furnace (via camera or probe).  
  - Exercise emergency shutdown and safety interlocks (functional test).  
  - Update calibration on temperature and pressure sensors.  
  *(Based on ISO 14224 equipment data recording recommendations.)*

- **Lubrication Schedules:**  
  - *Spindle Bearings:* Relubricate every 8 hours of operation or 1000 tons rolled (whichever first).  
  - *Hydraulic Systems:* Replace hydraulic oil every 5000 operating hours or 1 year. Clean/replace inline filters weekly.  
  - *Gearboxes:* Top off EP (extreme pressure) gear oil weekly; drain-and-fill every 2 years.  
  *(Typical industrial practice for heavy machinery.)*

- **Alarm Response Procedure (Example):**  
  1. Acknowledge alarm on HMI.  
  2. Check gauge/reading that triggered alarm.  
  3. If a critical parameter (e.g. overheat), initiate controlled shutdown.  
  4. Record alarm event in log; notify maintenance.  
  *(Follows EEMUA alarm management guidelines.)*

- **Condition Monitoring Procedure:**  
  - Set up continuous vibration or ultrasound sensors on all critical bearings (as per ISO 13373).  
  - Record readings daily into CMMS.  
  - Define alert levels (e.g. 75% of critical amplitude) and alarm levels (90%).  
  - If any reading exceeds alert, schedule immediate inspection (coupling check, imbalance, etc.).  
  *(Based on ISO 17359 risk-based approach and SKF balloting guidelines.)*
